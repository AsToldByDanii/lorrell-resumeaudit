Audit Overview

You present a strong career story — 8+ years of progressive retail and wireless sales management, a demonstrated ability to develop teams, exceed revenue goals, and run operations. You have the experience employers in sales leadership and training roles are looking for.

However, your resume in its current form is not optimized for modern Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) and reads more as a job description than a career achievement record. Several formatting and content upgrades are needed before it will consistently surface in digital hiring pipelines or make a strong impression in the first 6-second scan by a recruiter.

The good news: the raw material is excellent. This is a refinement and optimization project, not a rebuild. With targeted improvements, your resume can be positioned to compete at the Sales Director, Regional Trainer, or Operations Manager level.

Areas of Opportunity

▲ Opportunity

No Quantified Achievements

This is the single biggest gap. The resume lists responsibilities, not results. Modern hiring managers and ATS algorithms look for numbers: % revenue growth, team size managed, training completion rates, headcount reduced, etc. Every bullet should answer: "So what? What changed because of you?"

▲ Opportunity

Summary Needs Modernization

Your current summary is generic and could belong to almost anyone in retail management. It needs to be retargeted to your specific value proposition — who you are, what level you're targeting, and what makes you different from 200 other applicants.

▲ Opportunity

Skills Section Is Vague

The 8-bullet skills section lists soft skill categories ("Analytical Skills," "Excellent Communication") rather than ATS-scannable hard skills and tools. It needs specific platforms, methodologies, and measurable competencies added.

▲ Opportunity

Education Section Is Incomplete

SNHU and Long Island University are listed without degree completion status, graduation dates, or GPA. This raises a red flag for reviewers. Either clarify the status of each program (in progress, completed, or discontinued) or remove incomplete programs to avoid ambiguity.

▲ Opportunity

Verb Tense Inconsistency

Some bullets under Mobile Store Operators use present tense ("Provides," "Exceed") when they should be past tense since you're no longer in the role. This is a common oversight but a credibility signal to trained recruiters — it should be corrected throughout.

▲ Opportunity

Missing LinkedIn / Digital Presence

In 2025, a LinkedIn URL in the contact header is expected — not optional. Its absence can lead ATS systems and recruiters to skip over the profile. A polished LinkedIn that mirrors the resume should also be referenced directly.

▲ Opportunity

No Certifications Listed

8+ years in wireless retail with training responsibilities likely produced certifications (T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T authorized dealer certs, etc.). Any professional certifications in training (ATD, SHRM, etc.) should be added. If none exist, this is an investment worth making.

⚠ Critical

Bullet Under Luna Wireless Has Formatting Error

One bullet ("Collaborated with management teams…") begins mid-sentence without a bullet indicator and appears to be a continuation error. This will read as a typo to a recruiter and signals a lack of attention to detail — particularly damaging for a training professional.

2025 Resume Industry Trends

How your resume measures against what hiring professionals are prioritizing in 2025.

01
AI-Proof Specificity
Recruiters now distinguish AI-generated resumes by their generic language. Your resume currently sounds templated. Adding specific company outcomes, proprietary program names, and personal voice will set you apart.
02
Skills-First Prioritization
In 2025, many ATS systems and hiring platforms now weight skills sections equally with experience. Your skills section needs a full overhaul into hard, technical, and transferable skill categories.
03
Hybrid Role Readiness
Post-COVID job postings increasingly require candidates to signal comfort with virtual training, remote collaboration, and digital tools. Adding any LMS, video conferencing, or e-learning platforms you've used is now essential.
04
Micro-Credentials & Certs
Short-form certifications (LinkedIn Learning, Google Career Certificates, SHRM, ATD) have become accepted signals of professional development, especially where degree completion is in progress. These should be added immediately.
05
Personal Brand Alignment
Recruiters increasingly cross-reference resumes against LinkedIn profiles. If your LinkedIn doesn't reflect the same positioning and keywords as your resume, it creates doubt. Both documents need to align.
06
Storytelling Over Listing
The 2025 hire is someone who can demonstrate impact, not just activity. The shift from "I did tasks" to "here's what changed" is the single biggest differentiator between candidates at the same experience level.
